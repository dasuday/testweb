      About me
      ABOUT ME
      I an associate professor and program director in computer science. Dr. Das’s main area of research is Technical Language Processing (TLP). Current NLP approaches and LLMs are inadequate to dealing with the complexity of technical text that needs to be reasoned on in such a manner that the accuracy of the automated reading can be relied upon and the cross-referentiality of technical documentation can be captured.

      His current research is focused on developing higher reliability Technical Language Models (TLMs) which are essentially knowledge-graph backed LLMs that can pinpoint where information was drawn from within a complex information environment.

      Apart from that he is also keenly invested in ensuring that computing and technology is used for furthering social justice, and in challenging students to not only consider the harms as a result of computing technology, but to go beyond and consider how they could be designing and developing systems that always center justice and equity from the get go and throughout the process. This is inspired by such approaches as Design Justice and Values Sensitive Design but applied to a computing context.

      He is also constantly thinking about teaching methodologies and how to center fun in learning as well as removing barriers to access to CS education. In his spare time he likes reading, creative writing, bird photography and hiking.
